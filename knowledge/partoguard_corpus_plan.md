# PartoGuard Corpus Plan

Date created: 2026-05-10.

## Purpose

PartoGuard needs a safe corpus for four distinct uses:

1. **Form/layout knowledge** — blank partograph and Labour Care Guide templates.
2. **Educational examples** — filled training cases that show normal, Alert-zone, and Action-zone patterns.
3. **Synthetic image generation** — generated filled forms with machine-readable labels.
4. **Structured labour-progress distributions** — tabular labour datasets accessed through official data-use processes.

The corpus must not become a scraped collection of real patient records. Raw filled medical forms are high-risk by default.

## Core policy

The public repository may contain only:

- Citations, URLs, DOIs, source notes, and manifests.
- Schemas, extraction code, synthetic assets, and aggregate statistics.
- Assets with documented reuse rights and completed PHI/privacy review.
- Non-reversible derived annotations only when license and PHI review both allow them.

The public repository must not contain:

- Scraped real medical records.
- Raw filled partographs with unresolved identifiers.
- Access-controlled datasets or exports.
- Raw journal figures, screenshots, YouTube frames, or PDF pages without explicit reuse rights.
- Patient-level structured datasets such as NICHD DASH exports.

## Recommended corpus architecture

```text
Search Agent
  → URL Dedup Agent
  → Preflight Policy Gate
  → Source Ranker
  → Quarantine Downloader / Parser
  → Form Classifier
  → PHI + License Filter
  → Human Review Queue
  → Medical Knowledge Extractor
  → Dataset Builder
  → Final Manifest
```

Human review occurs before final dataset admission. Raw downloads never flow directly into a training/evaluation set.

## Storage tiers

| Tier | Allowed content | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| `open_repo` | Metadata, citations, manifests, code, schemas, synthetic assets, license-cleared/PHI-cleared derivatives | `knowledge/`, manifest rows, synthetic templates generated by us |
| `local_review_only` | Raw downloaded assets pending review or not licensed for redistribution | YouTube frames, journal figures, PDF page renders, real filled partographs |
| `rejected` | Items that fail PHI, access, licensing, or relevance checks | Identifiable patient records, access-controlled exports, unclear real records |

Safest practical dataset layers:

1. **Real knowledge layer:** WHO Labour Care Guide, WHO intrapartum-care documents, peer-reviewed studies.
2. **Form layer:** blank WHO/modified partograph templates and Labour Care Guide forms.
3. **Filled-example layer:** educational case studies plus synthetic filled forms generated from rules.
4. **Structured-data layer:** official datasets such as NICHD DASH / Consortium on Safe Labor, only through data-use agreements.

## Source ranking schema

```json
{
  "authority_score": 0,
  "license_score": 0,
  "medical_relevance_score": 0,
  "filled_form_likelihood": 0,
  "privacy_risk": 0,
  "reuse_allowed": false,
  "reason": ""
}
```

Suggested authority scoring:

- `5` = WHO, NIH/NICHD, HHS, government health ministry, peer-reviewed journal.
- `4` = university, hospital training program, major NGO.
- `3` = medical education site.
- `2` = public slide/document sharing site.
- `1` = unknown blog or copied image source.

Reject or quarantine if license or reuse rights are unclear for raw images/forms.

- `video_frame_or_screenshot`
- `real_filled_record`
- `unknown`
- `reject_privacy_risk`
- `reject_irrelevant`

Default `real_filled_record`, `video_frame_or_screenshot`, and `unknown` to `local_review_only` unless licensing and PHI review clearly pass.

## PHI/privacy screening baseline

Use a HIPAA-style identifier checklist as a conservative screening baseline, not as a legal de-identification determination.

Reject or quarantine if text/image/OCR contains:

- Patient name.
- Husband/father/relative name.
- Hospital number or medical record number.
- Phone number.
- Full address or small village/facility plus date.
- Exact admission, delivery, or discharge date.
- Signature.
- Facility stamp.
- Barcode or QR code.
- National ID.
- Clinician license number.
- Exact age plus rare diagnosis or unusual obstetric history.
- Any handwritten note that could identify a person or facility.

Output for every screened item:

```json
{
  "privacy_risk": 4,
  "detected_identifiers": ["exact date", "hospital number"],
  "action": "reject",
  "reason": "possible real patient record"
}
```

## Rejection criteria

Reject an item if any of these are true:

- It is behind login, paywall, CAPTCHA, request form, or other access control.
- robots.txt or source terms disallow automated fetching, reuse, or republication.
- It appears to be a real medical record and still contains unresolved identifiers, exact dates, facility names, or stamps.
- It is a copyrighted image/PDF/frame with no explicit redistribution right.
- Dataset terms prohibit redistribution, public posting, or model-training use.
- Clinical meaning is too ambiguous to use safely without human or clinician validation.

## Source-specific handling

- **YouTube frames:** local review-only by default. Store URL, timestamp, hash, and notes in the repo, not frame pixels.
- **Journal/PDF figures:** store citation and license evidence; raw figures stay local unless open license and PHI review pass.
- **Real filled partographs:** do not use for public training/evaluation without documented permission, successful redaction, and human approval.
- **NICHD DASH / CSL:** follow the DUA. Keep raw patient-level exports outside the repo; store only scripts/schema maps/aggregate outputs if allowed.
- **PartoMa:** promising educational source, but reuse rights are unclear for AI training. Contact the project before corpus inclusion.

## Human-review checklist

Before promoting an asset beyond `local_review_only`, reviewers must answer:

1. Is this blank, educational/synthetic, or a real patient record?
2. Are there names, exact dates, hospital numbers, facility stamps, signatures, barcodes, addresses, or other identifiers?
3. Is license/reuse evidence explicit and stored in the manifest?
4. Is redistribution allowed?
5. Is model-training or evaluation use allowed?
6. Does the item need clinician review before it contributes labels or interpretations?
7. If approved, what exact use is approved: `reference_only`, `training`, `evaluation`, or `knowledge_note`?

Fail closed when uncertain.
