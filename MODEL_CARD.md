# Model Card — PartoGuard Fine-Tuned Gemma 4

## Overview

PartoGuard uses a fine-tuned version of **Gemma 4 E2B-it** (2-billion parameter instruct variant)
by Google DeepMind for partograph mark extraction. The model takes a photograph of a WHO-style
partograph and extracts cervical dilation `X` mark coordinates as structured JSON.

## Intended Use

**Clinical decision support only.** The model extracts structured data points from
partograph photographs to assist trained healthcare workers in monitoring labour progress.

The model **does not make clinical decisions.** All clinical classifications are made
by a separate, deterministic rule engine (`partoguard/core/rules/engine.py`,
`android/app/.../rules/RuleEngine.kt`) that applies WHO partograph guidelines.

## Out-of-Scope Uses

- Autonomous clinical diagnosis or treatment recommendations
- Replacement of trained healthcare workers
- Use without human review and confirmation
- Deployment in high-stakes clinical settings without validation on real-world data

## Training Data

The V7 fine-tune was trained on 1030 synthetic partograph images generated by
`data/generate_corpus.py` and `scripts/generate_training_v2.py`. No real patient
data was used in training. See `DATA_LICENSES.md`.

## Evaluation

Evaluated on 350 synthetic images (separate from training data, seed 12345):

| Deployment | Accuracy | Latency |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Local bf16 (RTX 5090) | 350/350 = 100% | 5.77 s/image |
| VPS Q8_0 (llama.cpp/CPU) | 335/350 = 95.71% | 8.08 s/image |
| VPS bf16 (llama.cpp/CPU) | 336/350 = 96.00% | 10.09 s/image |

All failures are clinically safe (over-call to alert/action zones; no silent normal misses).

**Important:** Accuracy figures are on synthetic data only. Real-world clinical
accuracy requires validation on clinician-labelled real-world partograph images,
which has not been performed. Do not use these figures to support clinical claims.

## Limitations

- Trained on synthetic data only; real-world accuracy is unvalidated
- V7 has difficulty with real WHO chart templates (addressed by V8-WHO fine-tune)
- Dense charts (≥7 marks) have higher over-counting rate
- Rapid-precipitous curves have higher misclassification rate
